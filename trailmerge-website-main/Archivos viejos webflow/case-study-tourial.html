Tourial

Case Study

Trailmerge crafted a scalable design system and designed new features in partnership with this Atlanta-based startup team focused on improving the SaaS sales process.

In its first year of business, Tourial was already starting to make waves in the Atlanta startup ecosystem with its design tool geared towards SaaS marketing and sales teams. Several SaaS companies have used Tourial’s platform to build interactive product demos, enabling their sales teams to focus on closing deals with prospects who have already toured and expressed interest in purchasing their software.

If any startup understands product-led growth, it’s Tourial. This same vision led co-founder Andy Binkley to focus on user experience design for their own product early on. Knowing the first version of the Tourial platform required a high level of support from their team during the onboarding process, Andy had this to say:

“The trend towards product-led growth is obviously the first and foremost thing that we have to think about as a SaaS startup. It's all about making sure that we're thinking about the customer's experience first and failure to do that results in a tremendous amount of additional time spent onboarding and answering support tickets. That's the primary reason that we are investing to improve the UX of our product.”

When Andy engaged an embedded design team through Trailmerge, the task was clear: make the tool easier for new users to learn and adopt without the need for handholding from the Tourial team. The Trailmerge team started by conducting a thorough design audit, identifying detailed usability pain points and trends within the existing version of the app.

A couple of key needs that emerged from the audit were consistency (so users could more easily identify how to use different tools within the tour builder) and improved organization of the builder to improve comprehension and speed. The Trailmerge team set to work creating a design system of fresh, reusable Figma components and reorganized the builder interface to simplify and speed up users’ workflow.

Andy and the rest of the Tourial team particularly loved the value the new design system brought to the user experience and efficiency of Tourial’s development team:

“Everybody at Tourial is excited about the new design system. Now we have a color palette and consistent buttons, inputs and dropdowns. Now we have everything built in components and libraries for our developers, so they can just pull in that design module for this button or this drop down and it's all consistent.”

Six months after starting their collaboration with Trailmerge, Tourial’s customers are now using the newly-launched design system and v2 builder. Trailmerge continues to work alongside Tourial’s growing team to innovate on new features and incrementally improve existing features. Andy summed up his experience working with our team of product designers team like this:

“It’s clear that Trailmerge brings a lot of experience to the table because they are able to sift through a lot of noise and data to get to the design goals that we actually have without unnecessary meetings.”

If you’ve been looking to improve your SaaS product’s onboarding process, reduce the time invested in teaching your customers how to use your platform, or you’re wanting to differentiate your offering in a crowded vertical and ride product-led growth to success, Andy has some words of wisdom to share with you:

“The product-led growth wave is getting bigger and bigger by the day, and more and more companies that adopt it are immensely succeeding. It's very difficult to do and it requires a significant investment. One of those investments, arguably the biggest investment, in terms of time and resources has to be UX and design. If you get your UX right, you're going to lead your category, you're going to win your category, you're going to be the leader in that space.”
